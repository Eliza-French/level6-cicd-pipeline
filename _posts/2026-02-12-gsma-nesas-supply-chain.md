The Network Equipment Security Assurance Scheme (NESAS) is a [GSMA](https://www.gsma.com/old7865sds) initiative, run jointly with 3GPP, that audits how equipment vendors build and maintain the kit running our networks.

Two parts beloweds:

- **Vendor development audit** — an independent review of how a supplier writes, tests, and patches its products.
- **Product evaluation** — security testing of specific product categories against agreed baselines.

Why it matters now: regulators are increasingly citing NESAS as the floor expectation for procurement. The scheme is not perfect — it is audit-based, so it only goes as far as the auditor and the vendor's own honesty about its process. But it pulls procurement conversations away from glossy security marketing and toward documented, tested behaviour.

For DevOps practitioners in telco, the link is direct: NESAS expectations land on the same supply-chain controls that DevSecOps tooling already addresses — software bill of materials, reproducible builds, and signed releases.
